Sunshine: Stay Safe Out There

Spring and summer are just around the corner. As you start thinking about heading outside, planning family camping stays and day-trips to your favorite hiking spot, be sure you stay safe. Applying sunscreen of at least SPF 15 whenever you plan on being outside, can reduce the risk of skin cancer by 40-50%.

When the sun is shining (which we all love), it's easy to remember to stay covered. You grab your hat to keep the sun off your face, maybe long sleeves if it's not too hot to protect your arms. But no one really thinks about covering up when it's cloudy.

Cloud cover is deceptive. It makes us think we are in 'shade' and protected from the sun. Although light may be dimmer, the UV rays that can cause sunburn and increase the risk of skin cancer still pentrate through the clouds. Nothing can dampen a vacation faster than having to stay inside because your skin resembles the color of a stop sign, and it hurts to move.

So, stay safe. Cover up. And use sunscreen, so you can continue enjoying the outdoors for days on end without those horrible days suffering from sunburn.
